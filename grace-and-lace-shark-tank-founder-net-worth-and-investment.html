The Journey to Shark Tank

Before their appearance on Shark Tank, Melissa and Rick Hinnant embarked on a remarkable journey with their business, Grace and Lace. It all started with the simple idea of selling crochet cotton socks online.

The couple faced various challenges along the way, including the premature birth of their child. However, their determination and passion for their product led them to catch the attention of their neighbors and even strangers who were eager to purchase their unique socks.

“Grace and Lace started out of a simple act of kindness. A friend who was pregnant shared their sock secret with Melissa, and from there, it has truly touched millions of women’s hearts and legs.” – Rick Hinnant

Inspired by the demand and positive feedback, Rick suggested selling their socks online. This marked the beginning of their online business journey. As their brand gained traction, they expanded to retail locations, eventually establishing their presence in over 230 stores across the country.

“We grew from just sewing boot socks to boot socks with lace, leg warmers, scarves, arm warmers, and a whole line of boot socks for kids. We focus on adding intricate, feminine touches to our products that make them stand out.” – Melissa Hinnant

Their dedication to their craft and the quality of their products set them apart from the competition. This caught the attention of the producers of Shark Tank, and the Hinnants saw an opportunity to take their business to new heights.

With their sights set on expansion and the desire to reach a wider audience, Melissa and Rick decided to pitch their business on Shark Tank, hoping to secure an investment that would fuel their growth and propel Grace and Lace to even greater success.

In the next section, we’ll delve into the thrilling details of their Shark Tank pitch and the deal that changed the trajectory of Grace and Lace forever.

The Pitch and Deal

During their Shark Tank pitch, Melissa and Rick Hinnant presented their business, Grace and Lace, to the Sharks. They sought an investment of $175,000 for a 10% stake in their company.

The Sharks were immediately impressed by the strong sales numbers and the potential of Grace and Lace’s innovative products. Barbara Corcoran, in particular, saw the opportunity to partner with the Hinnants and make a significant impact in the fashion industry.

“I see great potential in Grace and Lace. The product is unique, and Melissa and Rick have proven that they know how to grow a business.”

Barbara made an initial offer of $87,500 for a 5% stake in the company. However, after some negotiation, she revised her offer to meet the Hinnants’ original ask of $175,000 for a 10% stake in the business, with half of the investment in credit.

The deal was a turning point for Grace and Lace. By accepting Barbara Corcoran’s offer, Melissa and Rick gained not only the financial support they needed but also a seasoned and successful business partner who believed in their vision. Barbara became their Shark Tank investor, propelling Grace and Lace to new heights of success.
