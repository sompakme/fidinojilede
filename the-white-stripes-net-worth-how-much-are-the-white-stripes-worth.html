Career with The White Stripes

In 1997, Jack White formed The White Stripes with his then-wife, Meg White. The duo signed with Italy Records and released their debut album, “White Stripes,” in 1999. They gained recognition with albums like “De Stijl” and “White Blood Cells,” which showcased their raw and energetic sound.

With their breakthrough albums, The White Stripes became influential figures in the garage rock revival of the 2000s. Their unique blend of blues-inspired rock and stripped-down instrumentation captured the attention of music enthusiasts worldwide. The band’s powerful performances and infectious energy brought the garage rock sound back into the mainstream.

“The White Stripes are one of the most important bands of the 21st century. Their raw and gritty approach to rock and roll inspired countless musicians and ignited a garage rock revival.” – Music critic

One of their most iconic songs, “Seven Nation Army,” became an international anthem, recognized for its catchy guitar riff and memorable lyrics. It solidified The White Stripes’ status as a household name and catapulted them to global success.

Throughout their career, The White Stripes released six studio albums, including “White Blood Cells,” “Elephant,” and “Icky Thump.” Each album showcased their evolution as musicians and further solidified their place in rock music history.
