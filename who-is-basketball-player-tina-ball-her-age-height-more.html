Biography Timeline

Tina Ball is an American entrepreneur and former professional basketball player. Born on December 12, 1967, in Riverside, California, Ball is best known as the mother of NBA players Lonzo, LiAngelo, and LaMelo Ball. However, she has also made a name for herself in business and sports.

1986-1991: Ball attended California State University, Los Angeles, where she played college basketball for the Golden Eagles.

1991: She was selected by the Philadelphia Rage in the inaugural season of the Women’s American Basketball Association (WABA).

1993: Ball joined the Richmond Rage of the American Basketball League (ABL).

1997: She retired from professional basketball and began her career as an educator.

2017: Ball appeared in the Facebook reality series “Ball in the Family,” which chronicled the lives of her family members.

2018: She launched the clothing and accessory brand “TB Balln,” which features products designed for basketball fans and players.

2020: Ball became the owner and operator of the Big Baller Brand, a sports apparel company founded by her sons Lonzo, LiAngelo, and LaMelo.

Despite her successful basketball career, Tina Ball has carved out a niche in the business world, using her experiences as a player and educator to create profitable brands. She has become a well-known figure in sports and entertainment, thanks in part to her involvement in “Ball in the Family” and the Big Baller Brand. With her continued success, Ball will remain a prominent figure in business and sports for years.
